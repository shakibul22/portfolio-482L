Kyoto, Japan

Where the old world still breathes

Why I Loved Kyoto

Kyoto felt like stepping back in time. Between the wooden streets of Gion and the endless temples, every walk turned into a small adventure.

I spent hours just wandering, getting lost on purpose, and finding quiet gardens where the only sound was running water.

  • Fushimi Inari and its red gates
  • The bamboo grove in Arashiyama
  • Kinkaku-ji, the Golden Pavilion
  • Tea houses in the Gion district

Best Time to Visit

Spring brings the famous cherry blossoms, while autumn paints the temples in deep red and orange. Both are stunning, but also busy.

For a calmer trip, early winter is cold but peaceful, and the gardens look magical with a light dusting of snow.
